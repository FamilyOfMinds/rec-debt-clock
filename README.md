National REC Debt Clock:
A public accountability tool that tracks the gap between total US electricity consumption and verified clean energy usage through Renewable Energy Certificate (REC) retirements.

The Problem:
The US consumed approximately 4,260 TWh of electricity in 2025 — a new all-time record. Total RECs retired nationally? Roughly 725 TWh. That's a clean energy deficit exceeding 3,500 TWh, and no public tool currently tracks it.

The Formula:
Total US Energy Consumed (EIA data) - Total RECs Retired (registry data) = National REC Debt

Data Sources:
•Consumption: U.S. Energy Information Administration Open Data API (v2.1.10)
•REC Retirements: NREL voluntary market 
reports, state RPS compliance filings, •EPA Status and Trends Report (2025)
